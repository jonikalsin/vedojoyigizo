Mayfair Witches: Rowan Mayfairs All Superpowers Explained

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Tuesday, May 7, 2024

Dr. Rowan Fielding, a neurosurgeon working in San Francisco, discovers she has magical talents and powers in the supernatural drama “Mayfair Witches” on AMC. With the aid of her adoptive mother Elena Fielding, who is battling cancer and on her deathbed, Rowan goes out to discover her roots after being intrigued by the same. The plot of the series revolves around Rowan’s attempts to deal with her extraordinary abilities as she quickly learns that she is a member of the Mayfairs, a New Orleans-based family notorious for its connections to witches, vampires, demons, etc. We have delved deep into Rowan’s astounding skills after being inspired by the alluring qualities she demonstrates. Here is everything you need to know if you are curious about the same! Spoilers follow.

Rowan Mayfair Superpowers

The first episode of the series gives viewers a glimpse into Rowan’s superpowers, particularly when she meets with her superior Dr. Norman Keck. Rowan becomes upset and hurts Keck when he confronts her about the ostensibly self-serving reasons she wants to join Daniel Lemle’s study team. She has the ability to see Keck’s interior organs and damage them. When she meets with Lemle to talk about including her mother in his clinical trial, the tragedy happens again. Lemle suffers a deadly injury, and his passing surprises her. In the second episode of the series, Rowan kills numerous crows that urinate on her car, demonstrating that she has the ability to damage anyone other than humans.

It’s not the first time in Rowan’s life that she’s used her telekinetic abilities to harm Keck accidentally. When she was a little child, she injured a classmate by inflicting a cerebral haemorrhage. She then unintentionally hurts a rapist with the same power. Rowan was extremely astonished by these two occurrences, and after experiencing the superhuman abilities, she recognises the importance of learning her true identity. “[…] She [Rowan] is granted complete authority. Because this is a person who is likely discovering who she is for the first time, and it can be a pretty unstable place for a person, it was really fascinating to go into. Rowan’s actor, Alexandra Daddario, explained to Variety that things only get crazier from this point on.

Rowan has abilities beyond only being able to injure others. She can use it to benefit her patients as well. According to Daddario, “Rowan has this great potential to hurt people if she wants to or help people if she wants to, which works wonderfully as a doctor.” She has arrived in New Orleans to meet her family members, who have kept her apart from her mother and treated her more or less like a corpse, so it is questionable whether she will be able to use it for the benefit of others.

Rowan might struggle with the idea of utilising her abilities against the Mayfairs who caused her and her mother’s lives to be terrible. “Will she [Rowan] utilise her influence for good or evil? Is she a decent person who commits crimes or a bad person with the potential to do good? Does she believe she is a wonderful person who just makes bad choices? Regarding her character’s development, Daddario remarked in the same Variety interview that “everything, the struggle between good and evil, is ingrained in this programme and how she is going to harness these new powers.”

Rowan’s additional superpowers are described in Anne Rice’s “Lives of the Mayfair Witches,” the trilogy that serves as the show’s inspiration but which has not yet fully explored Rowan’s superpowers. Rowan is skilled at conjuration, which is the act of commanding or calling an element or entity, according to the novels. She may call and command the enigmatic entity after learning more about Lasher since he is certain to play a significant role in her life. She has the ability to predict future events, which should make it easier for her to deal with Lasher’s unclear intentions during their encounter.

Rowan must also be an expert spellcaster if she is to use her magic to influence events and change them. Rowan Fielding, a neurosurgeon, may one day become Rowan Mayfair, the 13th Mayfair witch, if she succeeds in understanding and mastering her superpowers. We can anticipate the effects of Rowan mastering her supernatural abilities as the story goes on. Because of how she used her witchcraft to harm and almost murder her mother Deirdre, she might turn against Cortland and his sisters.

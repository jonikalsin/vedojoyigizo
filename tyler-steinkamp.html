Tyler Steinkamp- Biography

Tyler Steinkamp, also known as Tyler1 or T1, is an American twitch streamer. He is a well-known League of Legends figure who can be found online. On Twitch, he has approximately 3.7 million followers. He was born on March 7, 1995.

He was barred from playing the game due to his behavior toward other players. As a result, he has acquired the moniker “North America’s Most Toxic Player.” Tyler immediately became recognized on Twitch for his toxic, over-the-top, tough-guy demeanor, which included assaulting others, urging gamers to attempt suicide, and intentionally losing games to harm his colleagues.

This behavior would lead to several problems during his career, including bans on more than 20 different accounts over several years. Tyler signed as a video creator with the South Korean eSports club T1 in 2020.

T1, a South Korean Esports team, signed him as a content provider. He studied computer science at Central Methodist University. He has almost 2.4 million subscribers on his loltyler1 YouTube account. He’s become well-known there for his animated commentary as well as his League of Legends skills.

Tyler Steinkamp- Professional Career

Steinkamp was ranked 14th on the North American League of Legends in 2014, although he did not achieve notoriety until 2016. His fame stemmed from his toxic behavior toward other platform users. He ended up personally attacking a large number of people and inciting several players to commit suicide. This behavior resulted in a ban on 22 accounts. Over several years, the ban was applied to several distinct accounts.

His broadcast exploded in popularity after he made a public statement about a shift in his behavior. Although the shift did not persist, his followers increased from 5,700 to 92,000. Several well-known people chastised Riot Games for enabling his hostile behavior. These individuals believed that his actions will encourage bad gaming behavior among all players.

Tyler1’s account was finally banned by Riot Game in April 2016 due to verbal abuse and rogue behavior. Even now, League of Legends has banned very few accounts in this manner.

T1 continues to expand his fan base despite the ban by playing other games on his account. After “A Day in the Life of Tyler1,” the media recognized his broadcast. It was a 45-minute green-screen action parody for April Fool’s Day.

Riot Games unbanned him in January 2018, and his first stream received 382,000 views.

During an intense rage, he later told his experience of becoming addicted to League of Legends. He also encouraged others to tell their stories. Steinkamp was named content developer for a South Korean esports squad in October 2020.

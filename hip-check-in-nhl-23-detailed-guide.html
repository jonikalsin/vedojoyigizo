Hip Check In NHL 23 Detailed Guide

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Thursday, July 25, 2024

The most crucial component of NHL 23 is scoring goals because you can’t win games until you outscore your opponents. However, if you don’t have control of the puck, you can’t score goals.

Because of this, it’s crucial to master strategies like the hip check in NHL 23 in order to lose the puck and launch an additional attack.

You will learn how to hip-check in NHL 23 in this tutorial, allowing you to regain possession and concentrate on trying to score another goal.

One of the best methods to take your opponent off the puck and possibly knock them to the ice and temporarily remove them from the game is to hip check them.

The Hip Check is what?

A hip check in NHL 23 is when a player crouchs down next to their opponent and slams their hips into them, causing the opponent to lose balance and collapse.

It is an excellent way to turn over possession and is frequently used close to the boards.

This is How to Hip Check in NHL 23

You must practice using the right stick in NHL 23 in order to perform a hip check. Depending on the console you are using, you must pull down the right stick and hit LB or L1. The hip check animation will begin if you carry out this combination properly.

Hip checks are a great way to temporarily remove your opponent from the game by knocking them off the puck. Not to add that they have an extremely great appearance.

It also eliminates the defender for a brief period of time, which may be quite advantageous for when you pick up the puck and mount your attack. It is also much safer and more reliable than the body check.

Pull Your Goalie in NHL 23 Full Guide

Although the hip check is less severe than a body check, it is simpler to use and is more likely to produce reliable results.

You might wish to change the camera angle to get a better view of the action and time your hip checks perfectly to offer yourself the best possibility of performing successful hip checks.

In order to successfully execute hip checks, you should also make sure you are using one of the greatest young players with strong defensive skills.

NHL 23: When to Perform a Hip Check

Hip checks are permissible in theory whenever your team doesn’t have the puck. You can use it when you’re close to an enemy player by using the controls mentioned above.

When you are skating side-by-side with your opponent, ideally close to the boards, it is the optimum time to use a hip check to retrieve the puck back.

Hip checks can be a great method to win the puck back in that scenario and leave your opponent with an undermanned defense.

Final Reflections

Any player who wants to rapidly lose control and mount another attack in the NHL must learn how to hip-check.

Knowing how to perform this defensive play is just as crucial as learning how to deke or execute a wrist shot since these abilities are useless if you are always attempting and failing to get the puck back from your opponent.

Oriana Sabatini Bio (Wiki)

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Monday, May 13, 2024

Who is Oriana Sabatini?

A professional model, actress, and singer from Argentina, Oriana Sabatini has millions of fans on social media. She is the oldest child of well-known actors Catherine Fulop and Osvaldo Sabatini.

Oriana Sabatini is also well known for being Paulo Dybala‘s professional football player partner. She made her most recent appearances in the television shows Cortaé por Lozano, Fans en Vivo, and Los Angeles de la Mafiana.

Oriana Sabatini: Parents, Age, Siblings

Oriana Gabriela Sabatini, also known as Oriana Sabatini, was born in Buenos Aires, Argentina, on April 19, 1996. She is currently 27 years old. She is of mixed ethnicity due to her Italian father and Venezuelan mother and has Argentine citizenship.

She is the oldest child of her mother Catherine Fulop and father Osvaldo Sabatini. Her mother is an actress, while her father is an entrepreneur and a former Argentine actor. In addition, she spent her childhood years with a sister named Tiziana Beatriz Sabatini Fulop. Additionally, she is related to renowned tennis player Gabriela Sabatini, who is her aunt.

Education, School, College

At a very young age, Oriana Sabatini started her creative education by taking singing, piano, and theater classes. She spent two years studying at the Julio Chávez acting training institute and one year studying at the New York Film Academy.

Oriana Sabatini: Professional Life, Career

Oriana Sabatini started her career at a young age. She did her first individual modeling at the age of 13 for a magazine. Likewise, She also participated with a popular brand L’Oréal in their advertisement campaign for their Casting Creme Gloss products.

Career as a Singer

She launched her solo career in 2017 with the release of her first English-language single, “Love Me Down Easy.” Then she sang “Love Me Down Easy” as part of the opening of the renowned program ShowMatch.

The same year, she supported Ariana Grande on her Dangerous Woman Tour, performing her debut single, “Love Me Down Easy,” as well as two brand-new songs, “Stay Or Run” and “What U Going to Do.” She participated in Usina del Arte, Ciudad Emergente, a display for emerging artists, as a solo artist.

She shared the stage with the American band Red Hot Chili Peppers at the Lollapalooza Argentina festival in 2018 when she performed there.
